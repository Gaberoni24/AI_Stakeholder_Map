# AI Governance Stakeholder Map

**By Gabriel Sherman**

## What This Is

An interactive visualization of 270+ players in the AI governance ecosystem, plotted on a Power x Interest grid. The map helps answer a simple question: **who matters in AI governance, and how much do they care?**

Each stakeholder is scored on two dimensions:
- **Power (1-10):** How much influence does this player have over AI policy outcomes?
- **Interest (1-10):** How actively engaged are they in AI governance specifically?

These scores place each stakeholder into one of four quadrants:
- **Key Players** (high power, high interest) — the actors shaping policy right now
- **Keep Satisfied** (high power, low interest) — powerful but not yet fully engaged
- **Keep Informed** (low power, high interest) — deeply invested but limited leverage
- **Monitor** (low power, low interest) — on the periphery, for now

## Why This Exists

AI governance is moving fast and the landscape is fragmented. Government agencies, tech companies, civil society groups, international bodies, researchers, investors, labor unions, and individual voices are all jockeying for influence — but there's no single map of who's who and where they sit relative to each other.

This project attempts to fill that gap. It's not meant to be the final word — scores are AI-generated approximations based on publicly available information — but it's a starting point for anyone trying to understand the full picture: policymakers, researchers, advocates, journalists, or anyone working in or around AI.

## What's In the Data

The dataset covers 278 stakeholders across 19 categories:
- US Federal Government (Executive & Legislative branches)
- US State Governments
- International Governments & Regulatory Bodies
- International Multilateral Organizations
- AI Labs & Major Tech Companies
- Industry Groups & Trade Associations
- Civil Society, Advocacy & Nonprofits
- Think Tanks & Research Institutes
- Academic Centers & Programs
- AI Safety & Alignment Research Organizations
- Key Individual Voices & Thought Leaders
- Investors & Funders
- Labor & Worker Organizations
- Healthcare Sector
- Media & Information Ecosystem
- Affected but Underrepresented Populations
- Often-Overlooked Players
- Existing Mapping Resources

Each stakeholder includes a rationale explaining its scores and a source link.

## How It Works

The site is a static single-page React app. The raw data lives in an Excel spreadsheet (`AI_Governance_Stakeholder_Map_Scored.xlsx`) which is automatically converted to JSON at build time. The interactive scatterplot uses Plotly.js with zoom, pan, hover tooltips, and click-to-expand detail cards. Category filters on the sidebar let you isolate specific types of actors. A searchable directory below the plot provides full details on every stakeholder.

Labels on the plot appear progressively as you zoom in — a handful are visible at the default view, and more reveal themselves as you focus on a region, similar to how Google Maps works.
